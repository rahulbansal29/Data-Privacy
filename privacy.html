🔒 Privacy in Cryptography

1. Concept

Privacy in cryptography means protecting the content of information so that only authorized parties can access it.

  • Ensures that even if data is intercepted, it cannot be understood by unauthorized entities.
  • 👉 In other words: Privacy = Control over “what information” is revealed and “to whom.”

2. How Cryptography Achieves Privacy

  • Encryption:
    • 🔑 Symmetric: AES, ChaCha20 → Fast, same key for encryption & decryption.
    • 🔐 Asymmetric: RSA, ECC → Uses public/private key pairs.
  • Secure Channels: Protocols like TLS/SSL ensure private communication on the internet.
  • Homomorphic Encryption: Enables computation on encrypted data without exposing its content.
  • Differential Privacy: Hides individual data while still allowing aggregate analysis.
