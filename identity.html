Credentials

Credentials are proofs tied to identity that allow secure access, authorization, or trust in digital systems.

Roles

  • Issuer: Entity that provides credentials (e.g., a government or CA).
  • Holder: The person or device that stores credentials.
  • Verifier: Entity that checks the validity of credentials.

Types of Credentials

  • Knowledge: Something you know (password, PIN).
  • Possession: Something you have (smart card, hardware token).
  • Inherence: Something you are (biometrics like fingerprint, iris).

Modern Cryptographic Credentials

  • Certificates: Bind public keys to identities (X.509).
  • Verifiable Credentials: W3C standard for secure, shareable claims.
  • Tokens: JWTs, OAuth tokens, hardware-based tokens.
  • Anonymous Credentials: Prove rights without revealing full identity.
